About Beijing

Beijing, the capital of the People's Republic of China, a municipality directly under the Central Government, a national central city, a mega city, the national political center, cultural center, international exchange center, and scientific and technological innovation center, is the office of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, the Central People's Government of the People's Republic of China, the National People's Congress, and the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference.

Recommended Attractions

  1. The Forbidden City: The largest ancient architectural complex in China, it was the royal palace of the Ming and Qing dynasties, and it is also the largest and most complete wooden structure ancient building in the world today.
  2. The Great Wall: A World Cultural Heritage site, it is a symbol of China. The Great Wall is not only a magnificent defensive engineering, but also a symbol of ancient Chinese civilization.
  3. The Summer Palace: The most complete preserved royal garden in China, known as the "Royal Garden Museum", it is also the most complete preserved royal palace.
  4. The Temple of Heaven: The Temple of Heaven is the place where the emperors of the Ming and Qing dynasties worshipped heaven and prayed for the grain. It is one of the essence of ancient Chinese architectural art.
  5. The Bird's Nest: Beijing National Stadium, because of its unique design, it is called "Bird's Nest" by people. It was the main stadium of the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games.

Recommended Food

  • Beijing Roast Duck: Beijing's famous dish, renowned worldwide. Beijing roast duck uses high-quality Beijing duck and is cooked through a unique cooking process, making it rosy, tender, and unique in taste.
  • Zhajiangmian: Beijing's traditional noodle dish, delicious. The main ingredients of Zhajiangmian are soybean sauce and noodles, which are delicious and nutritious.
  • Douzhi'er Zhajiang: Beijing's traditional breakfast, unique taste. Douzhi'er Zhajiang is made from soybeans and fried sauce mixed together, with a unique taste and rich nutrition.
  • Lamb Spine Hot Pot: Beijing's traditional food, made from lamb spine, tender meat, and rich soup.
  • Sugar Huoshao: Beijing's traditional snack, with a crispy crust and sweet but not greasy filling.
